Real-World Waste-to-Energy Systems & Sustainability

pros

  • decreases methane emissions by diverting waste from landfills
  • if implemented on a smaller scale, can help remote/low income communities
  • reduces harmful waste that otherwise ends up in the ocean
  • reduce reliance on fossil fuels
    •

    cons

  • still carries risk of air pollution if not properly controlled
  • high cost-- requires skilled operation and frequent maintenance
  • energy efficiency varies-- some process are still less efficient than direct fossil fuel energy
  • produces ash, char, or tar that must be safely discarded
    •

    Examples:

    Enexor BioEnergy located in Tennessee-- gasification

    Dublin, Ireland Facility Poolbeg Incinerator -- incineration

    Brightmark Energy located in the U.S -- pyrolysis
